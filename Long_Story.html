'Resistance' Climbing Gym Software - 2021

I continued to use the Angular frame work + Flask to build a full featured climbing gym management software from scratch. The software has now been used for almost one year in Resistance Rock Climbing Gym, Dunedin, NZ.

The software was developed for some friends of mine, who had the plan of starting a new, bigger gym from a smaller gym they had operated for a long time. Initially they sought my advice on different softwares to acquire, but after comparing features and costs, I decided I could make a software much better suited for their needs for the same cost they might pay for an existing one.

Summer Student Research - Otago University Physics Department- 2018 Summer

I was lucky to have gotten a summer student research opportunity after one semester of study in computer science.

The physics department of my university had a professor who was looking for someone to write her some data manipulation tools to assist her research on climate science.

Her research involved understanding the dynamics of climate simulations and the tool was to be used to slice some Tera bytes of data according to certain criterias so she could 'zoom in' to different parts of the simulation to see what is going on.

I wrote a python program with an user interface which heavily relied on an external library 'netCDF4-python', which was essentially a single file and probably was many many many times slower than it could/should have been, but the professor was satisfied with my work and I completed the task with half of the time expected for the research student, as I was offered an internship at a local statistics consulting company.

This experience gave me more confidence in being able to write programs, and, in general confidence in my own ability to learn. I also learned about how science works, and what scientists do.

I attempted to use an LSTM model (with the help of Pytorch) to model the climate simulation itself, but to no avail. In general, I was introduced to concepts such as 1.Studying a certain phenomena by its simulation, 2.chaos (The reason for 1.), 3.Causality and how we might infer it from given information. Through this experience I began to understand a bit more what 'modeling' meant.

Reinforcement Learning, Evolutionary Algorithms and Anya - 2020

I became motivated to study 'reinforcement learning' as I had read the 'world models' paper by David Ha et Jurgen Schmidhuber(2018) and 'Curiosity-driven Exploration by Self-supervised Prediction' by Pathak et al (2017), where I became convinced that the human brain must also be doing forward prediction leads to learning world models.

I did some experimentations on the atari games and implemented DQN to solve space invaders and pong. I was unimpressed with how unstable the training could be with DQN, but I had understood the general principles behind reinforcement learning. I studied other 'methods' such as policy gradients, and many of the tricks to try get training to be a bit more stable, but I never implemented these myself, only running the code of others.

I also experimented with evolutionary algorithms, including evoutionary strategies, and NEAT. I ended up implementing NEAT and got it to solve Box2D Walker.

Later in the year the paper 'First Order Motion Model for Image Animation' came out and I knew all the ingredients for an audio to face speech system (ie.Animated Chatbot) was ready, so I put together some projects in some weeks and made a system which animated my girlfriend's drawing, which we named Anya. Soul Machines Internship - Early 2021

I worked on automatic animation of 3D human gestures at Soul Machines. Specifically, I created a prototype system for automatic pointing animations. So given a target point, the skeleton would animate so to point to that point.

Initially, people had been uploading files into auto desk maya to specify and visualize trajectories. I wrote a python script to do this automatically through TCP/IP sockets. But auto desk does not run their python scripts multi-threaded so if there was some trouble it will freeze, and things cannot be paused mid-way. So I wrote another visualizer based on Three.js that supports multiple skeletons asynchrnously. In the end of my internship I worked on understanding a recent paper called "Robust Motion Inbetweening". I did not implement it myself, but I had a solid understanding of the method and was able to modify it to our needs.

During this experience I learnt a lot on computer graphics, how artists work together with programmers to create things, and I also gained a deeper understanding of machine learning, modeling, and deep learning in general through more practical experiments.

I was offered after the internship a 4 days a week semi full time position, but I didn't accept it, and went back to university to do an honours year.
